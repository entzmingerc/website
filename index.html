About Me

I'm an engineer and musician. I enjoy teaching others how to make circuits and sounds. I've got a BS in Computer Engineering and a PhD in Electrical Engineering. My dissertation focused on real-time thermal modeling of SiC MOSFET power modules. I developed a technique which used reduced order thermal models and package surface temperature to identify faults in solder layers of power modules. On the music front, I've developed a script which turns a Monome Crow into a 4-voice synthesizer. I've also created a muscial sequencer app called Pit Orchisstra for Monome Norns based on the classic Snake game. My research work has been published in IEEE Journal on Power Electronics and my music has been released on a couple smaller record labels. In the past, I've worked at a national research lab, a renewable energy developer, and at tech companies.
